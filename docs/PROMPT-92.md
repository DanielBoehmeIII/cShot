# Prompt 92 — Find the Killer Demo

## The Killer Demo Script for cShot

### Concept

A 3-minute demo showing a producer going from stuck/generic to inspired/unique — in front of your eyes. The demo starts with the producer stuck because every kick in their library is wrong. It ends with them playing a beat built from 6 cShot-generated one-shots, smiling, reaching to open their DAW.

**Title:** "Stop Browsing. Start Making."

---

### Pre-Roll (15 seconds)

**Visual:** Split screen. Left side: Splice/Sample browser, thousands of files, scrolling endlessly, cursor clicking. Right side: cShot, clean dark UI, prompt bar blinking.

**Audio:** A half-finished beat loop plays. The kick is obviously placeholder — flabby, wrong key, wrong energy. The cycle repeats 3 times.

**Text overlay:** "The average producer spends 13 hours a week browsing samples."

---

### Scene 1 — The Problem (30 seconds)

**Visual:** Full screen — the producer's Splice account. Search "kick drum." 47,382 results. They scroll. Click play. Scroll. Click play. They audibly sigh. Try "punchy kick drum." 12,491 results. Same problem.

**Audio:** The placeholder kick loop. Slight EQ tweak. Still wrong. Another audition. Still wrong. Producer taps desk in frustration.

**Text overlay:** "What if you could describe the kick in your head — and get it in 5 seconds?"

**Producer says:** "I know what I want. I can hear it. But I can't find it in 50,000 samples."

---

### Scene 2 — The cShot Prompt (30 seconds)

**Visual:** cShot UI appears. Dark background. Single prompt bar at top. SoundGrid below (6 empty slots).

**Text overlay (morphing into the prompt bar):** "Describe the sound."

**Producer types:**
> "Tight 808 kick, punchy attack, round sub-bass tail, tuned to G, mix-ready"

**Visual:** As each word is typed, subtle suggestions appear below (semantic autocomplete): "808 kick," "punchy," "sub-bass," "tuned to G," "mix-ready." Producer selects "punchy attack" from a suggestion.

**Sound FX:** Satisfying click as they press Enter.

---

### Scene 3 — Generation (10 seconds)

**Visual:** The 6 slots fill one by one (waveforms animate in from left to right). Each slot shows:
- Waveform thumbnail (realtime-drawn SVG)
- SoundScore badge (e.g., "87 — Mix Ready")
- Estimated loudness (-6.2 dB LUFS)
- Spectral centroid indicator
- Duration (wall-clock time)

**Audio:** The 6 kicks play consecutively — each 2 beats apart. Each one is distinct: 1. round and warm, 2. aggressive and punchy, 3. short and tight, 4. deep and subby, 5. crispy with high transient, 6. the one that makes the producer blink.

**Producer reaction:** Subtle eyebrow raise on kick #3. A head nod on kick #5. Full smile on kick #6.

**Text overlay:** "6 unique kicks. 6 seconds. No browsing."

---

### Scene 4 — Refinement via Reference (30 seconds)

**Visual:** Producer drags a WAV file from their desktop into the ReferenceDropZone. It's a section of a track with a kick they love.

**cShot analyzes:** Waveform appears. Spectral display shows the frequency profile. "Analyzing reference..."

**Producer types:**
> "Same character, but snapier attack, less sub, key of D"

**Visual:** 6 new slots fill. The variation text appears on each: "Snapier attack, reduced sub, D tuning."

**Audio:** The reference kick plays once. Then 6 new variations play. The first one is beautifully different — same family, but tailored.

**Producer says:** "That's it. That's the one."

---

### Scene 5 — Waveform & Detail View (20 seconds)

**Visual:** Producer clicks on their chosen kick (slot #1). The DetailPanel opens:
- Full waveform with zoom
- Spectral display (frequency over time)
- Key metrics: transient time (23ms), spectral centroid (2.1kHz), RMS (-8.4 dB), crest factor, decay time
- SoundScore breakdown: Punch 92, Body 78, Clarity 88, Uniqueness 85
- Provenance card: generation parameters, model used, seed, date

**Producer hovers over "Punch 92":** tooltip explains: "Fast transient attack (23ms), high crest factor (14dB), strong 100-200Hz content."

**Text overlay:** "Full transparency. Full control."

---

### Scene 6 — One-Click Export (20 seconds)

**Visual:** Producer clicks the export button on the selected kick.

**Options dialog:**
- Format: WAV / AIFF / FLAC / MP3
- Bit depth: 16-bit / 24-bit / 32-bit float
- Sample rate: 44.1kHz / 48kHz / 96kHz
- Export to: Desktop folder / DAW browser folder / Splice folder (lol)
- With FX: include fades, normalization

**Producer clicks:** WAV, 24-bit, 44.1kHz → Export

**Visual:** A brief export animation. Progress: "Normalizing... Trimming silence... Writing WAV..." Done.

**Text overlay:** "File exported. 0.4 seconds. Ready to drop into your DAW."

**Brief cutaway to Finder/Explorer:** The file appears: `cShot_tight_808_kick_G.wav` — 24-bit, 44.1kHz, 342KB.

---

### Scene 7 — Pack Creation (20 seconds)

**Visual:** Producer navigates to the "New Pack" button. They see their current session:
- Kick (selected)
- They need a snare, hi-hat, 808 bass

**Producer types:**
> "Tight snare, crack attack, 200Hz body, short decay"

Generation. 6 slots. Snare #4 is chosen. Export.

> "Closed hi-hat, crisp, bright, 16th note feel"

Generation. 6 slots. Hi-hat #2 is chosen. Export.

**Visual:** The Pack view shows 3 sounds assembled. Producer names the pack: "Night Run Kit."

"Pack contains: Kick (24-bit, 44.1kHz), Snare (24-bit, 44.1kHz), Hi-hat (24-bit, 44.1kHz)"

**Text overlay:** "Your first custom pack. 3 minutes. 3 prompts. Done."

---

### Scene 8 — Before/After Payoff (30 seconds)

**Visual:** Split screen → Timeline view.

**Before (left side):** The placeholder beat. Flabby kick. Generic sound. The producer's frustrated face.

**After (right side):** The same beat, but the kick is the cShot kick. Snare cuts through. Hi-hat drives the rhythm. The whole mix sounds alive.

**Audio:** The "before" loop plays 4 bars. Then the "after" loop plays 4 bars. The difference is visceral. Punchier. Clearer. More exciting.

**Text overlay:**
> Before: Generic library kick. 47 minutes browsing.
> After: Custom cShot kick. 3 minutes. 3 prompts.

**Producer says (voiceover):** "I've been making beats for 8 years. I've never heard MY kicks until today."

---

### Scene 9 — Call to Action (15 seconds)

**Visual:** cShot UI fades to full-screen logo.

**Text (typewriter effect):**
> "You don't need a bigger sample library."
> "You need a sound generator that knows you."
> "cShot. The first one-shot imagination machine."

**Button:** "Join the Waitlist" (glowing gradient)

**Subtext:** "Beta access starts Q2. First 1000 producers get lifetime founder pricing."

**URL:** cshot.ai/waitlist

---

### Final Frame (5 seconds)

**Visual:** Simple. Logo. Tagline.

"cShot: Stop browsing. Start making."

Fade to black.

### Final Emotional Payoff

The demo should leave producers thinking:

1. **"I waste so much time browsing."** → Splice scrolling sequence is relatable pain.
2. **"That kick is exactly what I needed yesterday."** → Kick #3 is the hook.
3. **"Wait, I can use my own reference?"** → Reference workflow is the surprise delight.
4. **"I could build a whole kit in 3 minutes."** → Pack creation shows system value.
5. **"The before/after difference is insane."** → Audio comparison is the proof.
6. **"I would use this today. Right now. For real."** → Final emotional state. They reach for their wallet.

The demo is not a feature walkthrough. It's a story about a producer who stops being frustrated and starts being inspired. Every frame serves that arc.

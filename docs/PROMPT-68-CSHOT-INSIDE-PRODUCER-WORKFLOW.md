## 1. Beatmaker (Trap/Hip-Hop)

### Profile

```
Persona: Alex, 22, makes beats in Ableton Live.
Goal: Make 3 beats per day for YouTube/selling online.
Stack: Ableton Live 11, Splice subscription, MIDI keyboard, 2 years experience.
Output: 10-15 beats per week.
```

### Current Workflow

```
1. Open Ableton, load a drum rack
2. Spend 15-30 minutes browsing Splice for a kick
3. Scroll through 200+ kicks → preview fatigue → settle for "good enough"
4. Spend 10-20 minutes finding a snare that fits the kick
5. Layer 3 hi-hat samples to get the right groove
6. Spend 10 minutes processing samples to fit together (EQ, compression, saturation)
7. Finally start writing — 45+ minutes have passed before a single note is played
```

### Pain Points

| Pain | Frequency | Severity | Current Solution |
|------|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| Sample browsing time | Every session | High | "I just use the same kicks over and over" |
| Preview fatigue | Every session | Medium | "After 50 kicks they all sound the same" |
| Samples don't fit together | Often | High | "I spend more time EQing than writing" |
| Same sounds as everyone | Often | Medium | "Every trap beat has the same Splice kicks" |
| Export/import friction | Every use | Medium | "Switching apps kills my flow" |

### Where cShot Enters

```
Phase 1 — Web-to-DAW (current):
  Beatmaker opens cShot → types "punchy trap kick 140bpm"
  → 3 seconds later hears a kick unique to them
  → Clicks export → drags WAV into Ableton
  → It fits. No EQ needed. They write the beat.

Phase 2 — Plugin (future):
  No app switching. Generate kicks directly inside Ableton.
  Drag from plugin to track. DAW BPM auto-detected.
```

### Fastest Value Moment

```
"Generate a kick in 3 seconds that fits your beat immediately."

Time saved: 15-30 minutes per session.
Emotional impact: "I can't believe I got a better kick in 3 seconds
than I ever found on Splice in 30 minutes."
```

### Likely Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Can it do snares well?" | "Snares are our #1 improvement area. But our kicks are 4.2★ — start with those." |
| "I don't want another subscription" | "Free tier gives you 50 generations/month. That's 2 beats worth of kicks." |
| "AI sounds are never as good as real samples" | "Try a kick. If you don't love it, you lose nothing." |
| "I already have 10,000 samples" | "cShot is for when you need something NEW, not something you already have." |

### Required Integrations

| Integration | Why | Timeline |
|-------------|-----|----------|
| Ableton Live (via VST3) | Primary DAW | Phase 2 |
| FL Studio | 2nd most popular for beatmakers | Phase 2 |
| WAV export | Universal | Now |
| Splice (complement, not compete) | They use both | Never (don't integrate competitor) |

### Best Demo Use Case

```
"Show a beatmaker who's stuck: they have a melody but can't find a kick.
Open cShot, type 'punchy trap kick 140bpm', generate in 3s, drag into
Ableton. The kick fits perfectly. They start writing immediately.

The 45-minute sample hunt becomes a 5-second generation."
```

## 9. Cross-Persona Insights

### The Common Thread

Every persona shares one core pain: **finding the right sound takes too long.**

- Beatmakers: 30 minutes finding a kick
- Electronic producers: 4 hours designing a kick
- Film composers: 30% of scoring time is sound sourcing
- Game sound designers: days creating variations
- Sample-pack creators: 3 weeks per pack
- Pop producers: 4+ hours on drum selection

cShot doesn't replace sound design — it replaces the search.

### The Friction Hierarchy

```
Most painful (all personas):
  1. Browsing samples — "I need a kick and I need it now"
  2. Making samples fit — "Nothing ever works in my mix without processing"
  3. Getting enough variations — "One sound is never enough"
  4. Consistency across sounds — "My kicks and snares never match"
  5. Unique identity — "I sound like everyone else"

Least painful:
  6. Export/import — "Dragging a WAV is fine" (for now)
  7. Organization — "I'll name it later"
  8. Collaboration — "I work alone"
```

cShot enters the producer workflow at the exact point of maximum friction: **finding the right sound.** For every persona, the core loop is the same:
